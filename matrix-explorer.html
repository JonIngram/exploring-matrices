Matrix Explorer

There are 7⁴ = 2401 two-by-two matrices with integer entries in {−3, −2, −1, 0, 1, 2, 3}. For example:

Each dot on the screen is one of them. At the start they are arranged randomly, but you can organise them by choosing a mathematical property for each axis. As you change the axes, the dots animate to their new positions.

What you can do

  • Choose a preset for a guided view with an explanation
  • Use the X and Y axis dropdowns to build your own categorisation
  • Scroll and zoom to explore how matrices cluster together
  • Hover a dot to preview the matrix. Click it to see full details in the sidebar
